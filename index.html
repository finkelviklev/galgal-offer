07 · FAQ
07

Questions you might be having

Honest answers to the ones we hear most.

Does the price make sense and is it competitive?

Yes, and that's the rare part. You're hiring senior, hands-on builders who do the work themselves, not a layer of PMs and juniors. Most teams at this price either don't have the seniority, or do, but route the actual work down to cheaper hands.

The realistic alternatives for the same scope: top global agencies (IDEO, MetaLab, Work & Co.) run $400K–$1.5M+ for 8–12 person teams with account managers. Mid-tier US/EU consultancies run $200K–$450K with mixed seniority. Bigger isn't better, large teams spend ~3× the overhead just coordinating themselves. Offshore at $50K–$150K sounds cheap, but communication lag, a 1:8 senior-to-junior ratio, and rework typically mean paying twice within a year. Generalist agencies don't know the domain landmines: WhatsApp Business API approval timing, payment-provider marketplace compliance gates, Apple's "private community" review behaviour, Shabbat/holiday logic across time zones, each surprise costs 2–4 weeks. We've shipped social, marketplace, payment, and community products at Meta scale, and Elad has taken a company from zero to IPO.

I heard about vibe coding, can't it be done like that?

No professional would suggest building a real platform with vibe coding. "Vibe coding" means code that isn't monitored and fixed by an expert, it won't run at real load, won't scale, and can't be maintained. AI is a tool we use heavily, but AI alone can't make architecture decisions for payment-provider integrations, WhatsApp constraints, religious-schedule logic, safety, and scale. Same for no-code platforms (Bubble, Glide, Webflow), fine for landing pages, but they hit hard walls within ~3 months for a real marketplace and get rebuilt from scratch.

What if I want to stop halfway?

The only paid commitment to start is the Blueprint ($21,000, up to 2 weeks). After that you're on a monthly retainer and can stop at any point with 30 days written notice, both ways. No penalties.

Do I own the code, design, and brand?

Yes, everything. Source code, Figma files, brand assets, and all third-party accounts in your name. A future developer can take over with zero handoff drama.

How long would it take to onboard a new team if I want to switch later?

In the age of AI, fast. A competent team can pick up a well-built codebase quickly: they use AI to read the code and lean on the design and planning documents to hit the ground running. We deliberately build this way because it's simpler for us to keep maintaining what we build years later, we build as if for us, not to lock you in with us. Low switching cost is a feature, not a risk.

Can I speak with people who've worked with you and can recommend you?

Yes. We'll connect you directly with past partners and founders we've worked with, names, roles, and intro emails sent as soon as you'd like to talk to them.
